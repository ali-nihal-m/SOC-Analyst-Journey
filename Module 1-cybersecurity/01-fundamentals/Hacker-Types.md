# Types of Hackers

## Overview

Hackers are individuals who use technical knowledge to understand, test, or manipulate computer systems and networks. Hackers can be categorized based on their intent and authorization.

---

## 1. White Hat Hacker

### Definition

A White Hat Hacker is an ethical security professional who is authorized to identify and fix security vulnerabilities.

### Objectives

* Improve security
* Identify vulnerabilities
* Help organizations strengthen defenses

### Examples

* Penetration Testers
* Security Consultants
* SOC Analysts
* Security Engineers

---

## 2. Black Hat Hacker

### Definition

A Black Hat Hacker gains unauthorized access to systems for malicious purposes.

### Objectives

* Steal information
* Disrupt services
* Deploy malware
* Gain financial benefits

### Examples

* Ransomware operators
* Credential thieves
* Cybercriminal groups

---

## 3. Gray Hat Hacker

### Definition

A Gray Hat Hacker accesses systems without permission but may not have malicious intentions.

### Objectives

* Discover vulnerabilities
* Demonstrate security weaknesses
* Sometimes disclose findings to organizations

### Risks

Even without malicious intent, unauthorized access can still violate laws and policies.

---

## Comparison

| Type      | Authorization | Intent                  |
| --------- | ------------- | ----------------------- |
| White Hat | Authorized    | Improve Security        |
| Black Hat | Unauthorized  | Cause Harm or Profit    |
| Gray Hat  | Unauthorized  | Mixed or Unclear Intent |

---

## Key Takeaway

The difference between hacker types is not technical skill but authorization and intent.
